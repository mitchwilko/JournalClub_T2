# Overall concept

We can consider pair instability as the balance bewteen external photon pressure and internal gravitational pressure.
The collision between gamma rays and atomic nuclei will produce an electron positron pair. 
This will reduce the internal photon pressure which is required to hold up the supernova.

# Photon Pressure

High energy gamma rays are produced in the core of a massive star. 
If these emission reach sufficient energy, they will then begin to interact with the atomic nuclei and for our particle pairs.
Coming from the black body radiation laws, we know that the energy will increase as the temperature increase, thus the rate of reaction is proportional to the size.

# Pair Instability

This pair instability occurs as the above process forms a runaway effect, where the reaction rate is increased by the temperature which is increased by the collaps, and so on.

# Black hole formation through pair instability

The black hole mass gap can be explored seen when you investigate the method of supernova to black hole collapse as a fucntion of initial size.

1. Massive star, core collapse, black hole ~20-40 M~0~
2. More massive, Pulsational PISN, black hole ~ 40-50 M~0~
3. Even More massive, PISN, No black hole
4. Extremely massive, direct collapse after avoiding disruption, >120 M~0~
