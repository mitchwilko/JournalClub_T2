# The 12 C(α,γ) 16 O reaction

The position of the pair-instability mass gap depends not only on stellar mass loss and metallicity, but also on the 12 C(α,γ) 16 O helium-burning reaction. This reaction governs the conversion of carbon into oxygen during core helium burning, determining the mass and composition of the oxygen core that later experiences pair instability. Consequently, uncertainties in its rate translate directly into uncertainties in the predicted lower edge of the black-hole mass gap.
